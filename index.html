Haridwar to Rishikesh Yatra by Walk

Frequently Asked Questions about the spiritual journey on foot

The walk is approximately 20-25 kilometers depending on the route you take, which usually takes about 5 to 7 hours at a comfortable pace.

The ideal time is between September to March when the weather is cool and pleasant. Avoid the peak summer months due to the heat.

Some key stops include Muni Ki Reti, the banks of the Ganges, Lakshman Jhula, and various temples and ashrams along the route.

While generally safe, it is recommended to travel in groups or with a local guide, especially if you are unfamiliar with the area or walking during early morning or late evening hours.

Carry light water, snacks, comfortable walking shoes, a hat or scarf for sun protection, and basic first aid. Also, dress modestly respecting local customs.
